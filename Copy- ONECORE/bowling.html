Fast Bowling - Simon Dull

"Fast Bowling" in cricket is a style of bowling where the bowler delivers the cricket ball at a high speed or pace,typically exceeding 90 miles per hour (145 kilometers per hour) or more. Fast bowlers are an essential part of a cricket team's bowling attack and are known for their ability to generate speed, bounce, and swing with the cricket ball.

Slower Ball - Simon Dull

"Slower Ball" refers to a type of delivery bowled by a fast or medium-fast bowler with the intention of deceiving the batsman by reducing the pace of the ball. Slower balls are an essential variation in a fast bowler's repertoire and are typically used to disrupt the batsman's timing and induce mistakes.



Off Spin Masterclass - N Lyon

"Off-Spin" refers to a style of spin bowling where the bowler, typically a right-handed bowler, delivers the ball in such a way that it spins from the off side to the leg side (for a right-handed batsman). In other words, when the off-spinner releases the ball, it usually turns from the batsman's off side (the right side for a right-handed batsman) towards the leg side (the left side for a right-handed batsman).



Leg Spin Masterclass - CoachCricXI

"Leg Spin" refers to a style of spin bowling where the bowler, typically a right-handed bowler, delivers the ball in such a way that it spins from the leg side to the off side (for a right-handed batsman). In other words, when the leg-spinner releases the ball, it usually turns from the batsman's leg side (the left side for a right-handed batsman) towards the off side (the right side for a right-handed batsman).
